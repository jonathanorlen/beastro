Learning Model

At Aspire Onewe pride ourselves in having a dedicated Research and Content Development team who have designed QUATTRO. It is a unique 4-step Learning Model aimed at giving every student a smooth and streamlined learning experience. We implement a dynamic and effective process that guides the students and helps them identify their strengths and bridge their learning gaps.

Interactive lectures

At Aspire One our lectures are Interactive. This ensures a two way communication bridge between teachers and students. Besides providing new ideas and strategies, we also deliver different theories related to concepts by incorporating engagement triggers. This collaborative and inclusive engagement methods paired with appropriate content delivery mechanisms support understanding of concepts and maximum retention of lessons. It also enhances the value of the lectures and prevents boredom by placing the young minds on an accountable platform. Because our lectures consist of two way communication, it provides the teachers with an insight into the student's understanding and their thinking process relating to the concept under discussion.

Our Interactive lectures keep students engaged in classes

Tutorials

Our specially designed tutorials helps to groom critical thinking and comprehension skills of the students. This is where the concepts learned in lectures are applied. We provide specialized tutorial sheets for students to practice based on the concepts learned from the interactive lectures. It is a crucial aspect of the learning experience which helps to hone the problem-solving skills of the students. We take the time to understand and solve different problems using a variety of approaches. Tutorials are intended to help students gain a deeper understanding of the subject matter which enables their understanding to see the implications and significance of their knowledge and apply it the right contexts. It fosters a healthy relationship between the teachers and students, thereby personalizing the entire experience of the students and supporting their personal career development in general.

Tutorials ensure that students are well versed with the course content

Regular Assessments

Assessments are conducted at regular intervals which help students to achieve consistency, create a better understanding, test their grasp of concepts, and develop the mindset required to face exams. It goes a long way to fulfill our Vision of increasing the competition level among students. Besides helping the students, the assessments helps us to gauge the student's learning curve so that we can adjust our pace to their needs. It helps us to identify their strengths and weaknesses. Assessments involve an ongoing process of analysis and reflection on evidence, and to make consistent and informed judgments with an aim of improving the student’s future learning experiences.

Regular assessments are conducted to record the progress of the students

Feedback

A comprehensive analysis of assessments is provided to parents and students. This feedback clealy details the learning progress of the students. When imparting feedback to students and parents, we engage in dialogues which helps the students in the process of self assessment. This approach encourages learners to take responsibility for their learning. We are articulate and precise when providing feedback to ensure that parents and students are well informed and thus, enabling them to take the best corrective action as suited to their requirements.
With these coaching strategies, we ensure that quality learning practices are implemented accurately and taking our students to the limits of achievement.

Eligibility Criteria

A candidate, including a foreign national, must fulfill each and every one of the following five criteria to appear in (JEE Advanced) 2015.

Criterion 1Performance in (JEE Main) 2015.
The candidate should be among the top 1,50,000 (all categories included) in ( JEE Main) 2015.
The percentages of the total number of candidates for various categories is as follows: 50.5% for GEN (from the common merit list), 27% for OBC-NCL, 15% for SC and 7.5% for ST. Within each of these four categories, 3% horizontal reservation is available for PwD candidates.
Criterion 2 Age limit.
The candidate should have been born on or after October 1, 1990 if belonging to the GEN or OBC-NCL category and on or after October 1, 1985 if belonging to the SC, ST or PwD category.
Criterion 3 Number of attempts.
A candidate can attempt ( JEE Advanced) a maximum of two times and that too in consecutive years. Therefore, ( JEE Advanced) 2015 should be either the candidate’s FIRST attempt OR the SECOND consecutive attempt.
Criterion 4 Appearance in Class XII (or equivalent) examination.
The candidate should have appeared for the Class XII (or equivalent) examination for the first time in all the subjects in either 2014 or 2015.
Candidates who appeared for the Class XII (or equivalent) examination in 2014 and wish to re-appear for the same in 2015 (either for improvement or because they failed in one or more subjects), will have to re-appear in all the subjects in 2015. Those who appeared for the first time in their Class XII (or equivalent) examination in 2013 or earlier are NOT eligible. However, candidates whose Class XII (or equivalent) examination Board results for the academic year 2012-13 were declared after June 2013 are eligible to appear for ( JEE Advanced) 2015.
Criterion 5 Earlier admission at IITs/ISM.
The candidate should NOT have taken admission (irrespective of whether or not he/she continued in the program) OR even accepted admission by paying admission fee at any of the IITs or ISM in the past; even candidates whose admission was canceled are NOT eligible. However, candidates who have been admitted to a preparatory course in any of the IITs in 2014 can appear for ( JEE Advanced) 2015.
